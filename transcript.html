IS 270 Systems and Infrastructures

Hannah AlNemri, Lynelle Collo, Bob Emrich, Toni Wang

Interview with Bailey Berry

Librarian for Digital Conversion, Publishing, and Curation

Our interview with Bailey Berry was conducted on Thursday, February 26th, 2026 over the course of two Zoom calls. Bob was not present during the interview due to his class schedule conflicting with the availability Bailey had provided us. We were also slightly limited on time to conduct the interview due to Bailey’s own schedule. As Hannah worked with Bailey and is familiar with her job, Lynelle and Toni conducted the interview while Hannah took notes.

First Zoom Call

L: So first question just as an intro, what does your job entail on a daily basis?

B: Good question. It’s a lot. It’s a large mix of things. So my full title is Librarian for Digital Curation, Conversion, and Publishing. So I would say primarily I digitize items in our archives and put them online, I manage our digital collections website via Quartex, and we also handle a lot of scan requests from external researchers, things like that. And then I also manage the digital preservation of our digitized and born-digital items, and we use a platform called Preservica for that. And then I manage our Institutional Repository, which is through Digital Commons. So that’s all of our in-house journals get published there, like electronic theses and dissertations, all of that material. And then also I manage a bunch of kind of random digital projects, usually that end up being more like oral history projects. So the year I was hired, I had to finish up a grant project that was an oral history project.

[Interview paused while B took call]

I finished this grant project that was an oral history project, and I’ve been doing a lot of oral history work ever since.

L: You mentioned scan requests are these student requests or like outside patron researcher requests that are being made?

B: They are more- well both, mostly external researchers. We also get a lot of requests from the university marketing, or like alumni affairs or that sort of thing… So yeah, the majority of our requests are for researchers who want to access the materials. But we also get some like internal, more like marketing requests or dealing with alumni events, that sort of thing.
